# Social Media Strategy: C4A + CadreHealth + Maarova

## Three Brands, Distinct Audiences, Unified Voice

### Brand 1: Consult For Africa (@consultforafrica)

**Audience:** Hospital CEOs, medical directors, health system leaders, development agencies, diaspora healthcare professionals considering Africa

**Platforms (priority order):**
1. **LinkedIn** (primary) - Decision makers live here. Long-form authority content.
2. **Twitter/X** - Industry commentary, thought leadership, quick takes on healthcare policy
3. **Instagram** - Behind-the-scenes of engagements, team culture, event presence

**Content Pillars:**
- **Thought Leadership (40%)** - Nigerian healthcare market analysis, hospital performance insights, regulatory commentary (MDCN, NHIA, HMO policy), medical tourism trends
- **Case Studies & Results (25%)** - Client outcomes (anonymised where needed), before/after metrics, engagement spotlights
- **Team & Culture (15%)** - Consultant spotlights, "day in the life", diaspora consultants returning to serve, team retreats
- **Industry News & Commentary (10%)** - React to NMA announcements, healthcare investment news, brain drain data
- **Hiring & Network (10%)** - Open roles, consultant recruitment, partner firm spotlights

**Posting Cadence:**
- LinkedIn: 4x/week (Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri)
- Twitter/X: Daily (1-2 posts + engagement)
- Instagram: 3x/week (carousel, story, reel rotation)

**Voice:** Authoritative but warm. "We implement, we don't merely advise." Nigerian English, no jargon. Confident, not arrogant. Data-backed. Never generic consulting-speak.

**Key Metrics:** LinkedIn followers, post impressions, inbound enquiries from LinkedIn, demo requests, proposal requests traced to social

---

### Brand 2: CadreHealth (@CadreHealth)

**Audience:** Nigerian healthcare professionals (doctors, nurses, pharmacists, lab scientists, radiographers, etc.) - early to mid-career, 25-45

**Platforms (priority order):**
1. **Twitter/X** (primary) - Medical Twitter is massive in Nigeria. #MedTwitterNG, #NigerianDoctors
2. **Instagram** (primary) - Visual content, career tips, salary infographics
3. **TikTok** (growth) - Short-form career advice, "day in the life", salary reveals
4. **WhatsApp Channels** - Direct community engagement
5. **LinkedIn** - Professional positioning for senior professionals

**Content Pillars:**
- **Salary Intelligence (30%)** - "How much does a registrar in Lagos earn?", salary comparison infographics, CONMESS/CONHESS breakdowns, private vs public comparisons
- **Career Guidance (25%)** - Career readiness tips, interview prep, CV advice, exam guides (PLAB, IELTS, USMLE), migration pathways
- **Hospital Reviews & Transparency (20%)** - Anonymous hospital reviews, "things your hospital won't tell you", facility comparisons
- **Community & Identity (15%)** - Doctor memes (tasteful), professional pride, cadre-specific content, mentorship stories
- **Platform Features (10%)** - New features, claim your profile CTAs, testimonials from users

**Posting Cadence:**
- Twitter/X: 2-3x daily (mix of threads, quick takes, engagement)
- Instagram: 5x/week (3 feed posts, 5 stories, 2 reels)
- TikTok: 3x/week (short career advice, salary reveals, "did you know")
- LinkedIn: 2x/week (longer career guidance, industry analysis)

**Voice:** Peer-to-peer. Not lecturing, not corporate. Like a senior colleague who's been through it. Nigerian slang is okay in moderation. "Your career, your data, your power."

**Key Metrics:** Follower growth, profile claims (outreach conversion), platform signups, app installs, salary report submissions, hospital review submissions, engagement rate

**Content that performs in Nigerian Med Twitter:**
- Salary transparency posts (always go viral)
- "Which hospital pays better" comparisons
- Migration pathway threads
- "Things I wish I knew as a house officer"
- Professional development tips
- Exam preparation guides
- Hospital review summaries

### Brand 3: Maarova (@MaarovaHQ)

**Audience:** Hospital HR directors, medical directors, board chairs, nursing directors, health system leaders, development agencies

**Platforms (priority order):**
1. **LinkedIn** (primary and almost exclusive) - B2B buyers, HR professionals
2. **Twitter/X** (secondary) - Conference presence, thought leadership
3. **Email** (critical) - Nurture campaigns to leads

**Content Pillars:**
- **Workforce Crisis Data (35%)** - Brain drain statistics, retention data, turnover costs, "4,193 doctors left Nigeria in 2024" type posts
- **Assessment Science (25%)** - What psychometric assessment reveals about leadership, case studies of mis-hires prevented, EQ development results
- **Client Results (20%)** - "After implementing Maarova assessment, Hospital X reduced mis-hires by 33%", coaching outcomes
- **Product Education (10%)** - How assessment works, demo walkthroughs, feature spotlights
- **Thought Leadership (10%)** - African leadership development, Ubuntu philosophy in healthcare, cultural context in assessment

**Posting Cadence:**
- LinkedIn: 3x/week (data posts, case studies, thought leadership)
- Twitter/X: 2x/week (crisis data, event presence)
- Email: Monthly newsletter + event-triggered nurtures

**Voice:** Evidence-based, calm authority. "Built for Africa. Period." Not salesy. Let the data speak. Position as the only assessment tool built specifically for African healthcare context.

**Key Metrics:** Demo bookings, assessment completions, coaching programme starts, LinkedIn engagement from target titles (HR Director, Medical Director, CEO)

## Cross-Brand Synergies

**CadreHealth feeds C4A:** CadreHealth salary data and workforce insights become C4A thought leadership content. "Our data from 4,000+ professionals shows..."

**C4A feeds Maarova:** Client engagements generate Maarova case studies. "During our hospital turnaround engagement, Maarova assessment revealed..."

**CadreHealth feeds Maarova:** Professional development interest from CadreHealth users becomes Maarova lead generation. "Ready for leadership? Maarova can assess your readiness."

**Content Repurposing Flow:**
1. CadreHealth salary data > Instagram infographic
2. Same data > Twitter thread with analysis
3. Same data > LinkedIn article with C4A brand (industry insight)
4. Same data > Maarova context (why assessment matters for retention)

## Month 1 Launch Plan

**Week 1: Setup**
- Create all accounts
- Build brand kits in Canva
- Set up scheduling tools
- Audit existing followers and connections

**Week 2: Seed Content**
- 10 posts per brand queued
- First salary infographic for CadreHealth
- First case study for C4A
- First workforce crisis post for Maarova

**Week 3: Launch**
- Start posting on schedule
- Engage with relevant accounts daily
- Join relevant conversations (#MedTwitterNG, #HealthcareAfrica, #NigerianDoctors)

**Week 4: Measure & Adjust**
- Review analytics
- Double down on what performed
- Kill what didn't
- Plan Month 2 content calendar
