## 1. The market we are pricing into

We benchmarked against the contract the user pulled from the Cedarcrest engagement file (Purple Star Consulting, Nov 2024) and a sweep of Nigerian agency public data. Modal terms:

| Lever | Market norm | Purple Star (Cedarcrest) | Our position |
|---|---|---|---|
| Per-hire fee | 10% to 25% of first-year gross | **20%** flat | **15% to 20%** with tier logic |
| Replacement guarantee | 1 to 3 months | **3 months** (free re-recruit) | **3 months** standard, **6 months** on retainer |
| Delivery window | 8 to 12 weeks | **8 to 12 weeks** | **6 to 10 weeks** (we beat by 2 weeks on average; retainer = 4 to 6) |
| Payment terms | 10 to 30 working days | 10 days, 5% daily late interest | 14 working days, 1.5% monthly late interest (humane) |
| Withdrawal fee | 30% to 50% of would-be fee | **50%** of 20% (= 10% of role's annual gross) | **50%** of the would-be fee, mirror Purple Star |
| Out-of-pocket | client pays | client pays | client pays, pre-approved over NGN 50k |
| Retainer upfront | NGN 500k to NGN 2m for senior | not used | NGN 500k for retainer-tier roles, credited against fee |

Sources for the benchmarks: Profolio, Leonar, RecruitBPM, RemotePad recruiting-fee data; Nigeria-specific commentary from AfricaWork, Anthrop Management, NigeriaSearchGuide. The 20% rate is the modal anchor across mid-tier Lagos agencies; 25% is what Workforce Group and Stresert hold for executive search.

## 2. Why we go 15% to 20% (not 20% flat like Purple Star)

Three reasons.

**One. We compete on platform leverage, not effort.** Our sourcing cost-per-shortlist is lower because the CadreHealth platform already has the candidates registered, credential-checked, and salary-anchored. Pricing should reflect that, not match a generalist firm's hours-based logic.

**Two. We are unknown to these clients on the recruitment dimension specifically.** Pearl and Osiris know CFA as a consulting brand. Pricing 25% above market for an unproven service line is brand-burning. Better to anchor at parity with a slight discount, prove the model in 90 days, then move pricing up for engagement two.

**Three. We sell three tiers, not one.** A flat 20% across all role types ignores that a Dialysis Nurse hire (NGN 200k/month) at 20% nets NGN 480k, while a Consultant Nephrologist (NGN 1.5m/month) at 20% nets NGN 3.6m. The first economics are thin; the second are fat. We use tier and volume discounts to clear the thin ones at scale.

## 3. The three tiers

### Tier 1: Pay-Per-Hire (Standard contingency)

**For:** ad-hoc clinical and operational hires (Medical Officers, Nurses, Lab Scientists, Allied Health, mid-level admin).

**Fee:** 20% of first-year gross, before VAT.

**Discounts:**
- 18% if 3+ same-role hires in a single engagement (volume).
- 15% on a signed pilot (first 3 hires for a new client).
- 15% if client also subscribes to CadreHealth Connect.
- Floor: never below 15% on Tier 1. If a client pushes lower, move them to Tier 3 + skinnier per-hire economics.

**Guarantee:** free re-recruit if hire resigns within 90 days of start, voluntary resignations only.

**SLA:** first shortlist in 3 weeks, offer in 8 to 10 weeks.

**Billed:** on offer acceptance, NET 14 working days.

### Tier 2: Retainer (Engaged search)

**For:** scarce or strategic roles where contingency would underprice the work: Consultants, Heads of Department, Group CMOs, COOs, niche specialties (Nephrology, Oncology, Cardiothoracic).

**Fee:** 25% of first-year gross, before VAT.

**Upfront retainer:** NGN 500,000, credited 100% against the placement fee. Not refundable if the client withdraws the role.

**Guarantee:** free re-recruit if hire resigns within 6 months.

**SLA:** dedicated CFA partner, first shortlist in 2 weeks, offer in 4 to 6 weeks.

**Billed:** retainer on kickoff, balance on offer acceptance, NET 14.

### Tier 3: CadreHealth Connect (Annual subscription)

**For:** hospitals that prefer to self-recruit but want our reach and platform.

**Fee:** NGN 100,000 / year, before VAT.

**Includes:**
- Unlimited job postings on the CadreHealth job board for 12 months.
- A verified employer profile with hospital review surface.
- A curated candidate shortlist from the CadreHealth network on every posting (best 10 candidates by Career Readiness fit).
- Discounted per-hire rates if the client later moves a role to Tier 1 or Tier 2 (5 percentage-point discount; 20% becomes 15%, 25% becomes 20%).
- Access to a quarterly market salary report for their cadres.

**Anchoring rationale:** Jobberman's 2021 prices (likely 30% higher in 2026) are NGN 15,000 per single listing, NGN 75,000 for Best Match, NGN 129,000 for Pro-Recruit. We pitch a flat NGN 100,000/year for unlimited postings + employer branding + shortlist, which beats Jobberman on volume and rivals their value-add. Loss-leader pricing on purpose: it pulls hospitals onto the platform and seeds Tier 1 conversions.

## 5. Negotiation guardrails

**Hold firm on:**
- 15% floor on per-hire (anything lower and the unit economics break, given platform amortisation and screening cost).
- 14-day payment terms (Purple Star demands 10; we are humane but not casual).
- 50% withdrawal fee (mirrors industry; protects against tyre-kicking).
- Voluntary-resignation-only replacement (we cannot underwrite poor people management on the client's side).

**Concede if needed:**
- Move from NET 14 to NET 30 for trusted, slow-paying institutional clients.
- Waive the NGN 500k retainer for retainer-tier roles if the client signs a 3-role minimum at kickoff.
- Stretch SLA by 2 weeks if the role is genuinely niche (e.g. interventional nephrologist, paediatric oncologist) and supply is thin.
- Drop the CadreHealth Connect subscription fee for year one if it unlocks a multi-role retainer.

**Walk away from:**
- "Pay only on 6-month survival" clauses. We bill on offer acceptance; their job is to manage retention.
- Exclusivity demands across cadres they are not actively hiring for.
- Clients asking us to suppress the Career Readiness score, salary data, or hospital review surface from our candidate-side platform. The platform is non-negotiable.

## 7. Risks we are taking, and how we manage them

**Risk 1: We over-promise on the diaspora layer and under-deliver.** Diaspora candidates are real but slow. Mitigation: never include diaspora-sourced candidates in the 3-week shortlist promise. Diaspora is a Tier 2 (retainer) lever, with an explicit 8 to 12-week SLA caveat.

**Risk 2: Credential verification breaks at scale.** MDCN / NMCN APIs are unreliable. Mitigation: today we verify manually via the council registers; the engineering team owns automating this by end of Q3. Until then, every credential is checked by a human before shortlist.

**Risk 3: A pilot hire resigns within 90 days and we have to re-recruit for free.** This is the explicit warranty. Mitigation: salary-band rigour at intake (we walk away if the client lowballs); cultural fit screening in the assessment phase; document the offer terms so we can prove voluntary vs constructive dismissal cases.

**Risk 4: Pearl or Osiris expects CFA partner attention they are not paying for.** Mitigation: a named CadreHealth recruitment account lead (not a CFA partner) is the day-to-day owner. Partners surface only on retainer-tier engagements and the partnership conversation.
