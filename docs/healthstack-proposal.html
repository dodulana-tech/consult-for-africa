01Executive Summary

HealthStack Africa and Consult For Africa propose a joint pilot to deploy a national network of EMR and Digitization Advocates across Nigeria. These advocates will provide system-agnostic, on-the-ground support to healthcare facilities navigating the move from paper to digital.

The pilot will field four advocates with national remote coverage, each owning a defined geopolitical zone, supported centrally by C4A operations. The programme is structured to be commercially modest in Phase 1, prove operating mechanics over six months, then scale based on validated unit economics.

Headline ask

N13.32M for the six-month pilot covering four advocates, central coordination, and tooling. Expected output: 60 to 100 facility engagements, validated SOPs, and a scalable playbook for nationwide rollout.

02Strategic Context

Nigeria's health digitization gap is well documented. Despite Federal mandates, donor-funded EMR deployments, and the proliferation of vendor solutions, adoption remains patchy. The reasons are not technical. They are operational, behavioural, and human.

Most facilities lack:

  • A trusted advisor to guide system selection without a vendor agenda
  • Hands-on support through the messy middle of implementation
  • Continuity when external consultants leave or grants end
  • Peer-level connection to other facilities who have made the journey

HealthStack has built one of the more credible digital health platforms in Nigeria. Consult For Africa is the leading healthcare management consulting network on the continent, with a verified pool of clinicians and healthcare operators through CadreHealth and the C4A Agent Channel.

The opportunity: combine HealthStack's product credibility with C4A's distribution and human capital infrastructure to create a category-defining advocacy programme.

03Programme Overview

3.1 What is an EMR & Digitization Advocate?

An EMR & Digitization Advocate is a healthcare-literate, technology-fluent professional embedded within C4A's operational umbrella, deployed to engage healthcare facilities in their assigned zone. Their role is to:

  1. Identify and qualify facilities ready for digitization
  2. Conduct neutral, structured digital readiness assessments
  3. Recommend appropriate solutions (HealthStack and others) based on facility context
  4. Provide hands-on support through implementation
  5. Maintain ongoing relationships post-deployment
  6. Escalate to specialists when needed

Critically, advocates are positioned as trusted advisors, not vendor representatives. The system-agnostic stance is the differentiator and must be defended rigorously throughout the programme.

04Operating Model

4.1 Role Split

Consult For Africa is responsible for:

  • Recruitment, vetting, and onboarding of advocates
  • Employment contracts, statutory compliance, and payroll
  • Training curriculum design and delivery
  • Day-to-day operational management
  • Performance monitoring and SOP enforcement
  • CRM and reporting infrastructure
  • Quality assurance on facility engagements
  • Monthly invoicing and financial reporting to HealthStack

HealthStack Africa is responsible for:

  • Programme funding under agreed commercials
  • Subject-matter input into advocate training (HealthStack product, EMR landscape)
  • Lead handover of inbound HealthStack-aligned opportunities to advocates
  • Strategic direction and quarterly programme review
  • Co-branding approval and marketing alignment

4.2 Why C4A Manages the Operating Layer

Healthcare workforce deployment is operationally heavy. Recruiting, contracting, training, performance-managing, and paying field personnel across Nigeria is a discipline in its own right. C4A's CadreHealth platform already handles this for clinical placements. Layering advocacy on top is a natural extension.

It also preserves HealthStack's positioning. The advocacy programme is not a HealthStack sales channel. It is a neutral healthcare advisory function that happens to surface HealthStack as one option among many. That neutrality is strongest when the operating entity is not HealthStack itself.

5.2 Core Responsibilities

  1. Discovery and outreach. Identify priority facilities in assigned zone using CadreHealth data, public records, and partner referrals. Conduct first-contact engagements via phone, video, or in-person where warranted.
  2. Digital readiness assessment. Run a standardized 60 to 90 minute structured assessment covering: current workflows, infrastructure, staff readiness, leadership commitment, budget posture, and existing systems.
  3. Recommendation and roadmapping. Produce a written report for the facility outlining strengths, gaps, and a phased pathway. Include a shortlist of two to three system options matched to facility profile. HealthStack appears where genuinely fit-for-purpose.
  4. Implementation support. Where the facility selects a path forward, provide structured hand-holding through vendor engagement, configuration choices, training, and go-live. The advocate does not implement systems. They orchestrate.
  5. Post-deployment continuity. Maintain quarterly check-ins with engaged facilities for at least 12 months. Surface adoption issues, escalate to vendors, and track outcomes.
  6. Knowledge contribution. Submit weekly field intelligence to C4A. Participate in monthly cohort calls. Contribute to evolving SOPs.

5.3 Required Profile

  • Healthcare background (clinical or hospital management) with 3+ years experience
  • Demonstrated comfort with digital tools and systems thinking
  • Strong written and verbal communication in English
  • Local language fluency relevant to assigned zone (Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo, or Pidgin)
  • Self-directed, mobile, and willing to travel within zone
  • Reference-checkable and license-verified through CadreHealth

5.4 Disqualifying Affiliations

To preserve neutrality, advocates may not:

  • Hold equity, stipends, or referral arrangements with any EMR or HIS vendor
  • Concurrently work as a sales agent for any health technology product
  • Receive personal compensation from vendors during their tenure

This will be enforced through contract clauses, periodic attestations, and random audit.

07Standard Operating Procedures

7.1 SOP 1: Facility Identification and Qualification

Trigger: Weekly territory planning session.

Steps:

  1. Pull list of priority facilities in zone from CadreHealth facility database (filtered by bed count, ownership type, prior engagement status).
  2. Cross-reference with publicly available digitization signals (state grant lists, JCI/COHSASA pipeline, NHIA partnership status).
  3. Score and rank using the C4A Digital Readiness Lead Score (proprietary).
  4. Submit weekly engagement plan to Programme Manager every Monday by 12:00 WAT.

Output: Ranked list of 8 to 12 facilities for the week's outreach.

7.2 SOP 2: First Contact and Engagement Booking

Trigger: Facility appears on weekly engagement plan.

  1. Identify decision-maker (Medical Director, Hospital Administrator, IT lead, or owner).
  2. Reach out via the C4A-approved channel (warm referral, LinkedIn, association introduction, or cold WhatsApp).
  3. Use the standardized first-contact script (see Appendix A).
  4. Book a 60-minute discovery call within 14 days.
  5. Log every contact attempt in CadreHealth CRM.

Service standards: No more than three follow-up attempts per facility within a four-week window. After that, mark as cold and move on.

7.3 SOP 3: Digital Readiness Assessment

Trigger: Discovery call confirmed.

  1. Send pre-call briefing pack 48 hours in advance.
  2. Conduct structured 60 to 90 minute assessment using the C4A Digital Readiness Framework.
  3. Score across six dimensions: workflows, infrastructure, staff, leadership, budget, current systems.
  4. Within 5 business days, deliver a written assessment report (10 to 15 pages) including findings, score, and prioritized recommendations.
  5. Schedule follow-up to walk through the report.

Quality gate: Programme Manager reviews and approves every assessment report before delivery to facility.

7.4 SOP 4: Solution Recommendation

Trigger: Assessment complete, facility ready to consider options.

  1. Match facility profile to the C4A Solution Library (maintained by Programme Manager, includes HealthStack and competing systems).
  2. Identify two to three viable solutions across price points.
  3. Present options in a single comparison document. Never recommend a single system. Always present alternatives.
  4. Disclose any C4A affiliation with vendors in the recommendation footer.
  5. Hand off to facility for vendor engagement.

Neutrality safeguard: Quarterly external audit of recommendation patterns. If any single vendor exceeds 50% of recommendations, trigger root-cause review.

7.5 SOP 5: Implementation Support

Trigger: Facility selects a vendor.

  1. Co-develop a phased implementation plan with vendor and facility (12 to 16 weeks typical).
  2. Attend vendor kickoff meeting.
  3. Conduct fortnightly check-ins with facility through go-live.
  4. Surface and escalate blockers to Programme Manager and vendor.
  5. Conduct 30, 60, and 90-day post-go-live adoption reviews.

15Why This Will Work

This is not a sales programme dressed up as advisory. It is genuinely structured to be neutral, which is precisely what creates the strategic value for HealthStack. Facilities are tired of being sold to. They are starved for trustworthy guidance.

When advocates earn their trust, three things follow:

  1. Facilities self-select toward the systems best fit for them (HealthStack often wins on merit)
  2. Adoption rates rise because the implementation discipline is enforced by a neutral party
  3. The programme generates field intelligence that informs HealthStack product roadmap

Six months is enough time to prove this. Four advocates is the minimum viable footprint for national signal. C4A has the people, processes, and platform to make it happen.

AAppendix A: First Contact Script

To be developed jointly during induction. Below is a draft of the spirit and key talking points.

"Good afternoon, this is [Name] calling from Consult For Africa. We are running a national programme to support hospitals through the digitization journey, regardless of which system you eventually choose. Most facilities we speak with are weighing options, dealing with vendor pressure, or trying to make sense of where to start. We provide free initial consultations and work as your independent advisor through the process. Would you have 30 minutes next week for a structured conversation about where your facility currently sits on this journey?"

Key principles:

  • Lead with neutrality
  • Offer the consultation as free in pilot phase
  • Position as advisor, never as salesperson
  • Defer questions about specific systems to the assessment

BAppendix B: Digital Readiness Framework

The six assessment dimensions:

  1. Workflows. Patient registration, clinical documentation, billing, pharmacy, lab, imaging, discharge. Where is paper, where is digital, where is hybrid.
  2. Infrastructure. Internet reliability, power, hardware fleet, network, security baseline.
  3. Staff readiness. Digital literacy distribution, change appetite, prior system experience, training infrastructure.
  4. Leadership commitment. Executive sponsorship, board awareness, budget allocation, change management capacity.
  5. Budget posture. Capital available, operating expense tolerance, willingness to consider subscription models, donor or grant context.
  6. Current systems. What exists, what is used, what was abandoned, vendor relationships, data exportability.

Each dimension scores 0 to 10. Composite score determines readiness band: Not Ready, Foundational, Implementation Ready, Optimization, Advanced.
