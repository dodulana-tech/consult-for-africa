Thank you for the conversation. I have been thinking about it since.

The opportunity you described is one of the more strategically interesting ideas in Nigerian health digitization right now. There is a genuine vacuum between vendors who sell systems and consultants who advise on strategy. Facilities are stuck in the gap, and they need a trusted layer that sits with them through the messy middle.

The attached proposal sets out how Consult For Africa can stand up that layer with you, starting with a four-advocate pilot covering all six geopolitical zones. The headline points:

**Structure**

- Four full-time advocates, contracted and managed by C4A, providing system-agnostic advisory to facilities in their assigned zones
- Programme Manager (C4A) handles operations, training, performance, and reporting
- HealthStack provides funding, product expertise, and strategic direction

**Pilot scope**

- 6 months, May to October 2026
- National coverage by zone allocation
- Target: 60 to 100 facility engagements, 10 to 20 implementations supported
- Robust SOPs from day one (seven documented in the full proposal)

**Commercials**

- Total pilot cost: N13.32M, paid in three tranches (40 / 30 / 30)
- Per-advocate fully loaded cost of N400k/month including base, travel, tooling, and performance bonus
- 15% C4A management fee on top of direct costs

**Why this matters strategically for HealthStack**

The instinct will be to ask why you should fund neutral advisors when you already have a sales team. The answer is that the trust deficit in Nigerian healthcare digitization will not be solved by more sales. It will be solved when facilities have somewhere to go for honest guidance.

When advocates earn that trust, three things follow. Facilities self-select toward systems that genuinely fit their context, and HealthStack will frequently be that fit. Adoption rates rise because the implementation discipline is enforced by a neutral party. And the programme generates structured field intelligence that informs your product roadmap better than any survey.

Done well, this becomes the most credible health digitization advocacy programme in West Africa. That is a story worth telling and a category worth owning.
