# Haven Paediatric Centre

## Diagnostic Audit, Scope of Work
### Workstream 1 of the Consult for Africa engagement

Prepared by: Dr Debo Odulana, Founding Partner, Consult for Africa
For: Haven Paediatric Centre board and leadership
Duration: 4 weeks  ·  Fee: N2,100,000 (mobilisation, payable on signing)

---

## Objective

To establish, from evidence rather than impression, exactly where Haven stands across clinical safety, culture, operations, and cash, and to leave the board with a prioritised, costed plan of action. The audit is the foundation that shapes the detail of every workstream that follows. Two safety and cash quick wins are implemented during the audit, not after it.

---

## What we will examine

- **Clinical governance and patient safety.** Incident review including the recent mortality, crash-cart and emergency-readiness standards, clinical protocols, NICU readiness, and the safety routines that should run every shift.
- **Culture, routines, and incentives.** How work actually gets done day to day, where nursing and operational routines break down, and whether the current and proposed incentive tools (the staff commission structure, JDS and KPIs) reward quality and ownership.
- **Operations and SOP adherence.** Patient flow, customer service and booking, adherence to existing SOPs, and the gaps between what is documented and what happens.
- **Finance and working capital.** Revenue mix and yield (private versus HMO), margins, the roughly N4.2M in HMO receivables, the roughly N2.77M in pharmacy stock, and where cash is locked.
- **Procurement and inventory.** Stock management, procurement reliability, pharmacy margin, and the case for a vendor-managed inventory model.
- **HMO economics.** Contract terms, claims and ageing, and yield per HMO versus private patients.
- **People and structure.** Staffing ratio, roles, and the specification for the senior operations leader role.
- **Management reporting integrity.** Whether the numbers the board sees can be trusted, starting with the reconciliation gaps in the current report.

---

## How we will work

Structured interviews with leadership and frontline staff (nursing, pharmacy, customer service, operations), direct observation on site, review of financial, clinical, and operational records, and a short data request issued in week one. The approach is collaborative, not audit-by-ambush. The team is a partner in the findings.

---

## Quick wins delivered during the audit

- A stocked, sealed, checklist-governed crash cart with a shift-level check routine, live in week one.
- An immediate recovery push on the largest HMO receivables (Leadway and NEM), so cash starts moving before the audit even reports.

---

## Deliverables

1. **Operational and Clinical Governance Audit Report.** Findings across every area above, with prioritised, costed recommendations.
2. **Working Capital and Receivables Recovery Plan.** A concrete plan to unlock the cash currently tied up in receivables and stock.

Both are presented to the board in a working session at the end of week four.
